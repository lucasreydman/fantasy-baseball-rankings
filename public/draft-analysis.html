Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Rotation depth is unmatched — Crochet (Rank 6), Sale (22), Luzardo (40), Snell (80), Rasmussen (103). Five legitimate starters with real upside. Nobody in this league can match this staff top-to-bottom.
  • Cal Raleigh is a league-winner — At Rank 9 overall (VORP 8.26), Raleigh is the best catcher available and provides a massive edge over every team using a C ranked 95+.
  • Maikel Garcia is a secret weapon — 2B/3B/SS/OF eligibility means he slots anywhere. Multi-pos flexibility in a competitive league is priceless.
  • Brent Rooker in Round 4 — Rank 26 value in Round 4 is a strong pick. Power bat, proven producer.
  • Alex Bregman fills 3B quietly — Rank 58, taken at pick 83. Solid production with minimal injury risk at his age.
  • Blake Snell in Round 11 — Rank 80 value in R11 is excellent. Huge upside if he recaptures his Cy Young form.

Weaknesses

  • 1B is below-average — Perez has C/1B eligibility so he slides to 1B with Raleigh at C. Functional, but Perez (Rank 96) is one of the weaker 1B starters in the league. Most contenders have a Rank 25–75 1B. Upgradeable via trade, not a crisis.
  • Perez pick was still sub-optimal — Even with C/1B eligibility factored in, Seiya Suzuki (Rank 85, straight-up better player) was available at pick 110. You filled a need but at below-market value for a 10th-round pick. The pick wasn't wasted — it was just inefficient.
  • RP is dangerously thin — Only David Bednar. One injury and this category is toast. Most contenders carry 2–3 relievers.
  • SS is below-average — Dansby Swanson (Rank 126) is a starter-quality floor but not a ceiling play. Masyn Winn (Rank 171) as backup SS was a wasted 17th round pick.
  • Outfield depth is shallow — Marsee, Butler, and García are all fringe starters. A Taylor Ward slump creates real problems.
  • Over-indexed on pitching — 7 of 20 picks were pitchers. Elite, but leaves the hitting side thin in spots (1B, SS, OF depth).
